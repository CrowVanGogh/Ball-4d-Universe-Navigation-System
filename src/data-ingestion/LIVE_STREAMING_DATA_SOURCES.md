# Live Streaming Data Sources

This document provides comprehensive documentation of global harmonic and frequency data streaming sources, including:

### 1. NOAA Buoys
- **Description**: NOAA buoys are floating devices that provide real-time weather and ocean data.
- **Data Collected**: Wave height, wind speed, ocean temperatures, and atmospheric pressure.
- **Sources**: [NOAA National Data Buoy Center](https://www.ndbc.noaa.gov/)

### 2. Seismic Networks
- **Description**: Networks of seismographs that monitor earthquake activity.
- **Data Collected**: Ground movement, seismic waveforms, and frequency data related to seismic events.
- **Sources**: [United States Geological Survey (USGS)](https://www.usgs.gov/natural-hazards/earthquake-hazards)

### 3. Solar Monitoring
- **Description**: Systems that monitor solar activity and its effects on Earth.
- **Data Collected**: Solar flux, sunspot numbers, and solar wind data.
- **Sources**: [National Solar Observatory](https://www.nso.edu/)

### 4. Weather Stations
- **Description**: Stations that collect meteorological data across various regions.
- **Data Collected**: Temperature, humidity, precipitation, wind speed, and atmospheric pressure.
- **Sources**: [Weather Underground](https://www.wunderground.com/)

### 5. Ocean Data
- **Description**: Data sources that provide information on ocean conditions.
- **Data Collected**: Salinity, temperature, currents, and sea level.
- **Sources**: [Ocean Data Portal](https://ocean.data.ca.gov/)

### 6. Atmospheric Monitoring
- **Description**: Systems that collect data about the atmosphere and climate.
- **Data Collected**: Air quality, greenhouse gas concentrations, and atmospheric pressure changes.
- **Sources**: [National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)](https://www.noaa.gov/)

### Conclusion
These sources provide vital data for researchers, scientists, and decision-makers in understanding environmental changes and phenomena. Each source has its unique data and characteristics that contribute to a comprehensive understanding of global harmonic and frequency events.
